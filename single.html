When I was young, I loved to read books. Everywhere I went, I took my logic books and my GameBoy(for when I take a break from reading). I always had a passion for using my brain to figure out solutions to problems that I was faced with. I constantly tried to find ways to excercise the ole noodle. Through high school, I simply swallowed and regurgitated all the information that was thrown at me so I could graduate and just like anyone else, when I left, I dumped all of it.


After high school, I had no idea what I was going to do. So like any other young, arrogant idiot, I signed up for the military. And I didn't choose any of the simple ones, I chose the United States Marine Corps. The toughest of the tough. Five months after graduating high school, I was standing on the yellow footprints. Three months after that, I was a green-blooded Marine. While I won't go into details about my military career, I did learn a lot and about the nature of many people.


Fast-forward a few years to 2011. I got out of the military and made a few bad choices. But had things not worked out the way they did, I never would hae met my wife.
